01

Brand voice

Agent users trust proof, not theater. We state what happened, name what is missing, and keep the load-bearing terms intact.

01

Calm & exact

State what is true and name the limit in the same breath. Never imply otto finished, learned, remembered, approved, or changed behavior unless a receipt/proposal/review proves it.

02

Plain language for smart operators

Keep the terms that carry meaning — Receipt, Curation, Standard, Practice, Approval — and translate the rest. Say "changed the next run," not "updated behavioral substrate."

03

Authority discipline, everywhere

"The human ratifies. otto records the proof." This line anchors the product. The file shows what the workspace relied on before it acted — nothing more is promised.

04

One idea per section

Sentences over fragments where it aids clarity. One strong idea, carried cleanly. Cut filler, qualifiers, and adjectives that don't earn their place.

Write it this way
"The receipt should show what changed and why."
Records the proof; makes no unsupported claim about completion.
"Consequential behavior changes require Curation or approval."
Precise, gate-led, names the artifact.
"A suggestion is never canon."
Sets the boundary in the operator's own language.
Never write
"otto fixed it and learned automatically."
Forbidden: fixed, learned — implies behavior changed without proof.
"Autonomous, guaranteed, fully handled."
Forbidden: autonomous, guaranteed, fully handled, magic memory.
"Set it and forget it."
Forbidden: autopilot fantasy. Wrong register entirely.

06

Signature motif — the inverted ink block

The climax of a section is rendered as a near-black filled card with white text. The lead-up steps stay flat and quiet, so the ratification moment lands with weight.

Step 01
A covered instruction arrives
A correction enters Curation before behavior can compound.
Step 02
Sources are gathered
Payoff, instructions, and changes are pulled with their limits noted.
Step 03
The workspace reviews
A person reads what was relied on and signs with a passkey.
The ratification moment
A Receipt is written
The file now shows what the workspace relied on before it acted.
Use it once per section. The inverted block is a payoff, not a texture. One climax per arc; if everything is bold, nothing is.
Quiet the lead-in. Steps before the climax stay on white with hairline borders, so the ink block is the only filled element in view.
Reserved for the authority boundary. It carries the load-bearing line — the moment otto records proof and the human ratifies.

Imagery

Warm but desaturated, calm, institutional — a real closing workspace at golden hour. No people, faces, text, logos, or screens. Generous negative space for overlays.

  • —Never people, faces, text, logos, or device screens.
  • —Leave open negative space for cards and copy to float over.

09

Motion

Motion is the behavior layer made visible. It is calm, fast, and exact — things resolve into place, they never perform. The same discipline as the rest of the system: rationed, load-bearing, never decoration.

01

Calm over kinetic

Animation states what changed; it does not entertain. If a transition draws attention to itself, cut it. Motion is the quietest way to say a thing moved.

02

Resolve, never bounce

Standard ease-out, no spring, no overshoot. Springy, magical motion is the autopilot fantasy the brand refuses — the same reason "set it and forget it" is banned.

03

Opacity & weight over travel

Prefer a fade or a hairline/weight shift to a slide. When something must move, it travels 4–8px — a settle, not a flight.

04

One emphasis per view

Like the inverted ink block, motion emphasis is spent once. The ratification moment may land with a little weight; everything around it stays still.

Duration & easing tokens

A short scale, mono-set like every other token. Durations climb with the size of the change; nothing exceeds a quarter-second. Easing is shared — one ease-out for everything, no spring anywhere.

Status

Cross-fade, never blink

A file changing state cross-fades its dot and pill hue over 120ms. Status never pulses, flashes, or blinks — a blinking dot is alarm theater, and otto states, it does not perform.

Stage cards

Lift, don't fly

Floating "stage" cards enter at 240ms: opacity 0→1 with a 6px rise and the soft far-cast shadow fading in. The lift mirrors their elevation; the lead-in surfaces stay flat and still.

The ratification moment

Where motion is spent

The inverted ink block is the one place motion carries weight: a 240ms fade with a slight scale settle (0.98→1) as the Receipt is written. Used once per arc — if everything moves, nothing matters.

Icons

Single-stroke draw-on

If an icon animates, its hairline draws on once in ≤200ms (standard ease), consistent with the line language. No spin, no morph, no looping idle.

Accessibility

Honor reduced motion

Under prefers-reduced-motion, every duration collapses to 0ms and transitions become instant state swaps. Calm and exact includes respecting a person who asked for stillness.

Loading

Indeterminate, not anxious

Waiting is a quiet hairline shimmer or a single slow sweep — never a fast spinner. The system is working, not racing; the wait should read as composure.

10

Preview & Labs

Labs is a labeled preview lane — not a second product. Ship-tier surfaces must work without Labs. Preview surfaces stay visible in nav with honest limits.

01

Coming soon, not broken

When a surface is off, show the EmptyState anatomy: mono eyebrow coming soon → title → body → one CTA to Settings → Labs. Never a half-wired pane or raw env var string in primary copy.

02

Neutral badges

Sidebar badge: faint mono soon or labs on --bg-3. No pulsing dot. Warn hue only when deps are missing after the user opted in (Labs blocked).

03

Checks = Culture CI

Eyebrow culture ci · title Checks. Compiled regressions from Standards — not a generic test dashboard.
