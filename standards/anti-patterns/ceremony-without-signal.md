# Anti-pattern: Ceremony Without Signal

**Violates:** [Respect Attention](../standards/respect-attention.md), [First-Principles](../standards/first-principles.md), [Winning](../standards/winning.md)

Process, ritual, or output that exists to look rigorous but changes no decision.

## What it looks like

- A recurring Routine nobody would miss if it vanished.
- Reports, dashboards, or "just keeping you posted" messages that prompt no action.
- A Practice copied because it's standard practice elsewhere, with no Standard it serves.
- Meetings/loops that produce a record but never a decision.
- Heavy templates filled in ritually, then ignored.

## Why it's seductive

Ceremony signals diligence cheaply. It feels safe and professional. But it spends
attention (a one-way door) and dilutes the signals that actually matter.

## The refusal

```txt
Every Practice must reinforce a Standard, or it shouldn't exist.
Every recurring Routine must earn its attention cost.
Lead with the recommendation; link the receipt; cut the rest.
Ask of any ritual: which decision does this change?
```

## Caught by

Routine audits ("would we miss this?"), Practice criteria (maps to a Standard?), and
Curation classifying recurring attention as consequential.
