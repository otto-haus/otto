# Canon: Ben Horowitz — *What You Do Is Who You Are*

**Status: starting canon, not authority.**

Horowitz is the strongest available starting point for thinking about culture as
behavior. We study him seriously and steal what works. We do not treat him as a final
word.

```txt
External advice is input, not authority.
```

## Posture (beginner's mind, high bar)

```txt
Respect proven operators.
Study the canon seriously.
Do not cosplay expertise.
Do not cargo-cult.
Reason from first principles.
Adapt to our facts.
Grade outcomes over time.
```

Assume Horowitz has seen more than we have. Learn the canon carefully — then test every
claim against Otto receipts. Keep what bites; drop what doesn't.

## What we take from him

- **Culture is what you do, not what you say.** It is the set of actions your team takes
  when you're not in the room. This is the root of our `Culture = lived outcome` line and
  the reason Receipts, not slogans, are the proof of a Standard.
- **You are what you do under pressure.** A Standard only counts at the moment it's
  inconvenient. → our `under_pressure.{do,refuse}` schema.
- **Make rules that force the culture.** Concrete, surprising, hard-to-miss rules beat
  abstract virtues. → Standards are testable rules, not posters; Practices make them
  executable.
- **Walk the talk; the leader sets the floor.** What the founder tolerates becomes the
  standard. → Sebastian ratifies Standards; what Curation lets compound is the real culture.
- **Design from your virtues and your reality.** Borrow, but adapt to your actual
  situation. → first-principles Standard.

## Where we adapt (not copy)

- Horowitz writes for human orgs at scale. Otto is one human + an autonomous agent.
  Our "shocking rules" are **gates and receipts**, not war stories.
- His culture is enforced socially; ours is enforced by **Curation** (proposal +
  ratification) and file-based proof.
- He grades over years; we grade over **weeks** via the Standards review loop and
  Precedents.

## How this canon is used

- New or contested Standards may cite Horowitz in `related_*` or prose as supporting
  input.
- A citation never settles a debate. If a Horowitz principle conflicts with our receipts,
  the receipts win and we write a Precedent.
- Adding canon (a new book/operator) is itself a Standards change → Sebastian ratifies.
